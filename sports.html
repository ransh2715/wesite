Badminton

Badminton is a racquet sport played using racquets to hit a shuttlecock across a net. It can be played by two opposing players (singles) or two opposing pairs (doubles). The game has its origins in ancient civilizations, but the modern version of badminton has its roots in British India. It became popular internationally in the mid-20th century. Badminton is known for its fast-paced rallies and requires agility, speed, and precision.

The shuttlecock, also known as a birdie, is made from feathers or synthetic materials. Badminton is played indoors to prevent the wind from affecting the shuttlecock's flight. It is an Olympic sport and is widely enjoyed as a recreational activity as well.

Skipping

Skipping, also known as jump rope, is a cardiovascular exercise that involves jumping over a rope swinging over the ground. It is an ancient activity with a history dating back to ancient Egypt. Over the years, skipping has evolved and gained popularity as a form of exercise and a competitive sport.

Skipping ropes are commonly made of nylon, leather, or PVC. The activity improves cardiovascular health, coordination, and endurance. Skipping can be done individually or in groups, with various techniques and styles, including freestyle and double dutch.
